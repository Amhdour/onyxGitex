# Contractual Control Requirements (Priority 38 / Scope 314)

## Purpose
Define minimum contractual clauses required before onboarding AI-related vendors.

## Contract Minimums (Must-Have)
1. **Data access controls**
   - Vendor must enforce least privilege and tenant isolation for all customer data.
2. **Retention controls**
   - Data retention periods must be documented and configurable where feasible.
3. **Logging and auditability**
   - Security-relevant events must be logged and available to customer on request.
4. **Encryption commitments**
   - Encryption in transit and at rest is mandatory; key management responsibilities must be defined.
5. **Incident notification SLA**
   - Security incidents impacting customer data must be reported within contractually defined timelines.
6. **Subprocessor transparency**
   - Vendor must disclose subprocessors and provide notice before material changes.
7. **Audit rights**
   - Customer must have access to current independent assurance reports and right to request follow-up evidence.
8. **Exit/deletion obligations**
   - Vendor must support data export and certified deletion at termination.

## Evidence Required Before Signature
- Current SOC 2 Type II and/or ISO 27001 evidence.
- Executed DPA with security and privacy terms.
- Incident response commitments with explicit timelines.
- Data retention/deletion policy mapped to service data types.
- Subprocessor register and notification process.
- Security contact and escalation path.

## Approval Rule
- Legal/Procurement cannot mark controls complete unless required evidence is attached to the procurement record.
- Missing evidence must be tracked as a contractual blocker or documented risk acceptance.

## Exceptions Management
- Exceptions require:
  - business justification,
  - security compensating control,
  - named risk owner,
  - expiration date,
  - revalidation checkpoint.
