# Service Handoff Checklist

## Purpose
Confirm operational, security, and governance readiness before transition from project team to steady-state owners.

## Handoff Details
- Client:
- Service name:
- Planned handoff date:
- Service owner:
- Security owner:
- Operations owner:

## Checklist

### 1) Documentation and Knowledge Transfer
- [ ] Architecture, data flow, and trust boundaries documented.
- [ ] Runbook and escalation paths delivered to operations.
- [ ] Control ownership matrix approved.
- [ ] Incident response contacts validated.

### 2) Control and Monitoring Readiness
- [ ] Required controls verified or formally risk accepted.
- [ ] Audit logging enabled and retained per policy.
- [ ] Alert routing tested with named responders.
- [ ] Access review cadence scheduled.

### 3) Open Findings and Remediation
- [ ] All open findings listed with owner and due date.
- [ ] Accepted risks documented with approver and expiry date.
- [ ] Post-handoff remediation meetings scheduled.

### 4) Launch Gate and Decision Evidence
- [ ] Launch gate criteria review completed.
- [ ] Evidence pack delivered (tests, logs, configs, decisions).
- [ ] Leadership decision recorded: Go / Go with Risk Acceptance / No-Go.

### 5) Post-Handoff Governance
- [ ] First 30/60/90-day review dates set.
- [ ] Continuous review KPIs defined.
- [ ] Change control process agreed.

## Evidence Limitation Language (Client-Facing)
Handoff confirms the state of evidence and controls at transition time only. Newly introduced integrations, policy changes, or architecture changes may alter risk and require reassessment.

## Launch Gate Decision Language (Client-Facing)
Handoff completion does not override launch gate requirements. If required evidence is missing or material findings remain unresolved, the appropriate outcome is **No-Go** or **Go with Risk Acceptance** under documented authority.
