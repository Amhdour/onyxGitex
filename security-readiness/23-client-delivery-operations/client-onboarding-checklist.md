# Client Onboarding Checklist

## Purpose
Use this checklist to start each client engagement with clear scope, evidence expectations, and delivery controls.

## Onboarding Steps

### 1) Contract and Governance Setup
- [ ] Confirm signed SOW, MSA, and NDA (if applicable).
- [ ] Confirm data handling responsibilities by party.
- [ ] Confirm decision owners for scope, risk acceptance, and launch gate decisions.
- [ ] Confirm communication channels (email, ticketing, secure file share).

### 2) Scope and Constraints
- [ ] Confirm in-scope systems, data sources, and user groups.
- [ ] Confirm out-of-scope systems and assumptions.
- [ ] Confirm jurisdiction and regulatory context (e.g., privacy, sector obligations).
- [ ] Confirm timeline, milestones, and escalation path.

### 3) Access and Evidence Readiness
- [ ] Confirm read-only access needed for assessment activities.
- [ ] Confirm log and audit export capabilities.
- [ ] Confirm document inventory locations and owners.
- [ ] Confirm evidence retention location and naming standard.

### 4) Risk and Testing Alignment
- [ ] Confirm risk rating model and severity definitions.
- [ ] Confirm test windows and client blackout periods.
- [ ] Confirm incident reporting expectations during testing.
- [ ] Confirm remediation SLA targets by severity.

### 5) Launch Gate Alignment
- [ ] Confirm launch gate criteria and decision authority.
- [ ] Confirm that unresolved High/Critical findings require explicit risk acceptance.
- [ ] Confirm required evidence package format for launch gate review.

## Evidence Limitation Language (Client-Facing)
This engagement assesses controls and behaviors based on the evidence provided, systems made accessible, and tests executed during the agreed period. Results are point-in-time and may not detect all possible weaknesses. Any areas without sufficient evidence will be labeled **Unknown** or **Partially Confirmed**.

## Launch Gate Decision Language (Client-Facing)
Launch gate status is based on verified evidence available at decision time. A launch recommendation is not a guarantee of incident-free operation. If material controls remain unverified or significant findings remain open, the recommendation should be **No-Go** or **Go with documented risk acceptance**.
