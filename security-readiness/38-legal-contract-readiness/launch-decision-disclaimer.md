# Launch Decision Disclaimer

> **Non-Legal Draft Notice (Required Lawyer Review):**
> This is draft operational language and **not legal advice**. Legal counsel must review before inclusion in any binding launch or acceptance document.

## Purpose
Provide conservative disclaimer language for launch-gate decisions based on readiness evidence.

## Template Language
"A launch decision indicates that, based on currently available evidence and documented assumptions, stakeholders determined residual risk to be acceptable for the approved scope at the time of decision.

A launch decision does **not** constitute:
1. A warranty of security, compliance, or uninterrupted service.
2. Confirmation that all vulnerabilities, abuse paths, or failure modes have been identified.
3. A determination that future changes will remain within accepted risk.
4. A substitute for continuous monitoring, reassessment, and governance.

If material changes occur (including architecture changes, model/provider changes, new data domains, policy changes, incident trends, or threat landscape shifts), risk acceptance must be re-evaluated and launch status may be suspended pending review.

Where evidence is incomplete, uncertain, or stale, decisions should default to conservative outcomes, including delayed launch, scoped rollout, compensating controls, or explicit executive risk acceptance." 

## Decision Hygiene Checklist
- Evidence recency validated.
- Open high-severity findings reviewed.
- Unknown and Partially Confirmed controls explicitly logged.
- Risk owners and approvers identified.
- Reassessment trigger conditions documented.

## Operational Notes
- Pair this disclaimer with dated decision records.
- Require explicit sign-off authority mapping.
- Retain evidence references for audit.
