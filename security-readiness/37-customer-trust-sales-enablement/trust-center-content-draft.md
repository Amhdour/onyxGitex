# Trust Center Content Draft

_Last updated: 2026-05-11_

## Headline
AI Trust & Security: Evidence-Backed Readiness for Internal Knowledge Assistants

## Short Intro
Our AI readiness program is designed to help organizations evaluate and deploy internal knowledge assistants with strong security discipline, conservative claims, and transparent evidence.

## What We Prioritize
- Preventing unauthorized data retrieval,
- Reducing unsupported responses,
- Enforcing policy decisions with fail-closed intent,
- Preserving auditability for investigation and governance.

## How We Validate
We use a lifecycle-based readiness approach covering scope, threat modeling, control design, observability, testing, and evidence acceptance. Readiness statements are tied to concrete artifacts: code/config references, tests, logs, and documented assumptions.

## Evidence Language We Use
- **Verified**: Observed and reproducible evidence exists.
- **Partially Confirmed**: Evidence exists with known gaps.
- **Unknown**: Insufficient confirmation at this time.

## Important Boundaries
- We do not claim guaranteed safety.
- We do not claim zero risk.
- We do not claim certification in this draft.
- Evidence and readiness posture are time-bound and environment-specific.

## For Security Reviewers
Available materials may include:
- Security FAQ,
- Client evidence summary,
- Control and testing summaries,
- Residual risk and governance artifacts.
